The Ticking Time Bomb of Climate Change

Americans are starting to see the results of the warming of our planet. What will need to happen before people take action to change their daily habits contributing to the warming, and boycott or protest current investments known to emit CO2 and greenhouse gasses.

Social Implications

Maybe climate change seems far off in the future, or that it's inveitable and you might as well not work to solve the problem.
Here are a list of things that global warming will change for us socially in the near future:

  1. It may be too hot to hangout outside, in seasons other than summer.
  2. Natural disasters such as drought, flooding, wildfires, hurricanes etc. will happen more frequently.
  3. Drought can harm food production.
  4. Water levels rise and wipe out costal homes and businesses
In addition to the personal and societal effects that we will face due to climate change, the economics and money that runs our would will also have implications.

Economical Implications

The results of all these little changes to our world build up and can impact the economy in a couple of different ways. Natural disasters will result in the need for more financial allocation to prevention and impact. Insurance against natural disasters may skyrocket as it would be in high demand.
